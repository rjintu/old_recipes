Green Pea Cutlet

Serving Size: 10 cutlets

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup peas
  • 2 large potatoes
  • 2-3 green chilis
  • ½ cup bread crumbs
  • Salt (to taste)
  • ½ tsp mango powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • ½ tsp cumin and coriander powder
  • ½ cup oil
  • ¼ cup finely chopped green coriander leaves

Directions:

  1. Grind peas, green chili, and ginger to a coarse paste (don’t make very smooth).
  2. In a pan, heat 2 tbsp oil and add mustard seeds, asafoetida (Hing), turmeric powder in the pan. After the seeds start popping, add the ground mixture from step 1.
  3. One by one, add mango powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and coriander leaves. Mix well in the pan. Add salt (to taste) and after mixing well, turn off the pan.
  4. Make small round balls from this mixture for the cutlet filling.
  5. Separately, boil potatoes, mash them, and add salt (to taste).
  6. red chili powder and 3 tbsp bread crumbs to the potato mixture from the previous step. Make small bowl with potato mixture. Put the potato mixture in the bowl, then close the bowl and flatten to form the cutlet.
  7. Pour the rest of the bread crumbs into a flat plate. Coat each of the cutlets with the bread crumbs.
  8. Lightly shallow fry each cutlet in the pan.
  9. Cutlet is ready! Repeat steps 11-12 for any remaining filling and dough.


Basundi

Serving Size: 2 people

Ingredients:

  • 1 stick of butter, melted (ghee)
  • 2 cups full milk
  • 2 tablespoons 2% milk
  • 3 tablespoons milk powder
  • ¼ cup powdered sugar
  • A pinch of saffron
  • A pinch of cardamom powder
  • Cashews, pistachios, and/or almonds, sliced (for garnishing only)

Directions:

  1. Grease a nonstick pan with butter/ghee.
  2. Put the pan on the stove and pour full milk on it.
  3. Boil the milk until it becomes thick.
  4. Mix milk powder with lukewarm 2 percent milk for five minutes or until thick.
  5. Pour the mixture from Step 4 in the boiled milk, add sugar and saffron. Stir for a few minutes.
  6. Shut off the gas and cool the mixture for 5 minutes.
  7. Mix it for two minutes.
  8. Garnish the Basundi with cardamom powder and dry nuts.
  9. Enjoy! Basundi is ready to eat!


Shrikhand

Serving Size: 2-3 people

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • ¼ cup powdered sugar
  • 2 teaspoons milk
  • A pinch of saffron
  • Nuts and/or mango, sliced and cut up (for garnishing only)
  • A pinch of nutmeg powder
  • ½ teaspoon cardamom powder

Directions:

  1. Bind yogurt in a thin clean cloth for 4-5 hours at room temperature.
  2. Mix the yogurt 1-2 minutes, and then mix in powdered sugar.
  3. Warm the milk in the microwave for 10 seconds.
  4. Add saffron to the milk and use a finger to mix it in.
  5. Mix the milk from step 4 in the yogurt.
  6. Add the garnish (cut nuts and mango) to the yogurt mixture as desired.
  7. Add cardamom powder and nutmeg powder to the yogurt mixture.
  8. Enjoy! Shrikhand is ready to eat.
